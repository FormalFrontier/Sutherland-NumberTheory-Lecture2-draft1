One of our first goals in this course is to prove that the ring of integers of number fields and coordinate rings of global function fields are Dedekind domains. More precisely, we will prove that if $A$ is a Dedekind domain and $L$ is a finite separable extension of its fraction field, then the integral closure of $A$ in $L$ is a Dedekind domain. This includes the two main cases of interest to us, in which either $A = \mathbb{Z}$ and $L$ is a number field, or $A = \mathbb{F}_q[t]$ and $L$ is a global function field. Recall from Lecture 1 that number fields and global function fields are the two types of *global fields* (as we will prove in later lectures).
