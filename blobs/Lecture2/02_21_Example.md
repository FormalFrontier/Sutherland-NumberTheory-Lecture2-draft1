**Example 2.21.** Consider the subring $A := \mathbb{Z} + 2i\mathbb{Z}$ of the Gaussian integers (with $i^2 = -1$). The set $I := 2\mathbb{Z}[i]$ is a non-invertible $A$-ideal (even though it is an invertible $\mathbb{Z}[i]$-ideal); indeed, we have $A \div I = \mathbb{Z}[i]$ and $I(A \div I) = 2\mathbb{Z}[i] \subsetneq A$.
