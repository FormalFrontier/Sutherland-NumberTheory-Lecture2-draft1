An important special case of localization occurs when $\mathfrak{p}$ is a prime ideal in an integral domain $A$, and $S = A - \mathfrak{p}$ (the complement of the set $\mathfrak{p}$ in the set $A$). In this case it is customary to denote $S^{-1}A$ by

$$A_{\mathfrak{p}} := \{a/b : a \in A, b \notin \mathfrak{p}\} / \sim, \tag{3}$$

and call it the *localization of $A$ at $\mathfrak{p}$*. The prime ideals of $A_{\mathfrak{p}}$ are then in bijection with the prime ideals of $A$ that are contained in $\mathfrak{p}$. It follows that $\mathfrak{p}A_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is the unique maximal ideal of $A_{\mathfrak{p}}$ and $A_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is therefore a local ring (whence the term *localization*).
